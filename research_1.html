Model Fingerprinting for Copyright Protection

As large language models (LLMs) become increasingly widespread, protecting their intellectual property has emerged as a critical concern. Unlike text watermarking, which only traces generated outputs, model fingerprinting secures the model itself by embedding or extracting unique, verifiable signatures. These fingerprints enable ownership attribution and misuse detection, even if the model is leaked or redistributed. Our research explores two complementary approaches: intrinsic fingerprinting, which derives signatures from inherent model properties, and invasive fingerprinting, which embeds externally defined signals into model weights or behaviors. By evaluating these methods across dimensions such as robustness, stealthiness, and reliability, we aim to provide systematic solutions for verifying model provenance and strengthening copyright protection in the era of LLMs.
